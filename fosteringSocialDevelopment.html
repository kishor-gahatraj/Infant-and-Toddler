Fostering Social Development

Introduction of Fostering Social Development

Early childhood social development is the process through which young children learn how to communicate with others, control their emotions, and cultivate social skills. These abilities include the capacity for good communication, sharing, taking turns, and empathy. (Gordon & Browne, 2022). Understanding social standards and developing relationships with classmates, adults, and even siblings are other aspects of social development. Teaching competencies and skills: 1.Educators can build a welcoming and warm learning community by demonstrating respect, listening skills and positive expectations about respectful and caring behaviour (Essa & Burnham, 2019). Use everyday interactions as learning opportunities 2.Promote deeper thinking by creating an environment where children feel safe can assist them to state their opinions and reasons (Sheridan et al., 2020). 3.Encourage children to use e ective verbal and non-verbal communication skills while interacting within the learning community (Gordon & Browne, 2022). 4.By discussing moral issues and listening to a range of views, children and young people can build awareness and understanding of diversity

Authentic Curriculum plan and examples
Art:
Activities like Collaborative painting or group collage helps promote sharing and teamwork, develop to work cooperatively, have a patience to take a turn. Children learn to share materials with their friend and respect other ideas (Sheridan et al., 2020). Drama and puppetry:
Dramatic play scenario encourages children to play cooperatively, understand roles and responsibilities. Children can develop communication skills. Through imaginative play they can reflect real life experiences. Children can learn practicing social scripts like greetings, asking for help (Mooney, 2017; Gordon & Browne, 2022).
Mathematics and Numeracy
To develop children math skills through action we can set up activity like playing board game. This activity develops number sense and counting skills. Helps children understand basic addition and subtraction through movement. Also, they learn the concepts of more or less and in order (Essa & Burnham, 2019).
Movement and music
Group dance
For the activity we need open space, age-appropriate music and some props like ribbons for expressive dance. The objective of this activity is to build confidence of children and practice self-regulation, patience, and empathy in a group setting. The activity promotes cooperation as well (Berk & Meyers, 2018).
Science
Group investigation of a nature topic
It is a flexible activity for children where they explore outdoor area looks at the nature, collect bugs and share their findings. From this activity they are connected to nature. They develop their observation skills by exploring around the living things (Gordon & Browne, 2022).

Learning experiences


1. For 0-12 months


Experience: Peekaboo with a scarf
Activity: Educator can cover face and say, ‘where did I go?’ then uncover yourself and say “Peekaboo”.
Outcome: This activity encourages attention and joy of child. This activity also promotes attachment and enhances social skills like eye contact, fostering trust and connection with their caregivers.

2. For 12-24 months


Experience: Musical instrument circle
Activity: In this activity we can use some musical instruments like shakers and make a circle. After that we can pass the instruments around the circle. So, each child gets turn to play and pass it to another child.
Outcome: This activity makes children aware of being fair to each other and giving respect. It also teaches children to have a patience while waiting for their turn to play.

3. For 2 to 3 years

Experience: Feelings Faces Emotion matching game
Activity: In this activity we can show a card with di erent kind of emotion like happy, sad, angry and ask children to match them how they are feeling or act out. Outcome: This activity encourages children about verbal and non-verbal communication about feelings. Children learn to communicate using facial expressions and words. Children can recognise di erent kind of emotion that the others are expressing.
