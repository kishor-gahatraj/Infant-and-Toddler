Introduction of Supporting Cognitive Development

Understanding cognitive development in Early Years Cognitive development refers to the growth and maturation of all thinking processes, including perceiving, concept formation, remembering, problem-solving, imaging and reasoning. Cognitive development means how children think, explore and figure things out. It is the development of knowledge, skills, problem solving and dispositions, which help children to think about and understand the world around them. Brain development is part of cognitive development. Cognitive Development in Infants and Toddlers Cognitive development of children in the first years of life is complex. During their first three years of life, children learn faster than they will ever learn again. They are actively collecting and arranging information about their environment. Young children learn about their social surroundings by engaging with others. They start to grasp basic concepts through their senses: seeing, touching, hearing, smelling, tasting, and moving (Sheridan et al., 2020). Toddlers become more aware of details and distinctions in their environment and develop an interest in more intentional play (Gordon & Browne, 2022). Piaget and Vygotsky are two well-known theorists of cognitive development. According to Piaget theory he suggests that suggests that children's intelligence undergoes changes as they grow. Whereas Vygotsky theory suggest that learning does not happen in isolation:instead, it is influenced by social interactions with more knowledgeable others, such as parents, teachers, or peers (Mooney, 2017)

1. Birth to 0 to12 months

Experiences: Treasure Basket Exploration
This activity can be applicable for birth to 12 months especially ideal for 6 to 12 months. This activity supports babies' cognitive development by providing multisensory experience which encourages exploration for babies. The variety of things in the basket helps them to develop their concentration skills. It also develops babies hand eye coordination and fine motor skills. It o ers the opportunity to stimulate all the babies five senses.
