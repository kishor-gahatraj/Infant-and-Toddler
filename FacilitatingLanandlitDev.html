Facilitating Language and Literacy Development

Introduction of Facilitating Language and Literacy Development

Infants along with toddlers experience maximum growth in language proficiency from environments that provide ample opportunities for speaking and listening in addition to conversing tasks which promote language abilities and literacy abilities. Listening sessions that include reading and meaningful conversations combined with developmental experiences of early reading and dialogic learning build future literacy abilities (Berk & Meyers, 2023; Whitehurst et al., 2021)

Authentic curriculum examples

Art: Sponge painting


The art activity allows children to work with big sheets of paper and primary colors during which they try sponge painting techniques. The creative painting activity enhances children's sensory development as well as their hand-eye coordination and lets them show their individuality. This activity allows toddlers to explore artistic freedom during their creative experiments with both colors and textures according to Edwards (2022).
Languages: Shared Reading book
When the teacher read Brown Bear, Brown Bear, What Do You See to the class it enhanced listening skills as well as language comprehension and future thinking capabilities. Children build basic reading confidence and phonological awareness skills when they both repeat and predict phrases according to Whitebread & O'Sullivan (2021).
Science: Exploring sensory
Children during their toddler stage observe ice cubes as they melt gradually throughout periods of time. The physical interaction with ice cubes helps children develop questions about changes they observe alongside predictive abilities and knowledge of relationships between factors. Sensory activities enable children to form basic scientific understanding while becoming curious about transformation in nature (Blaisdell et al., 2022).
Movement:
The soft indoor course provides toddlers with climbing and crawling opportunities through slides. The obstacle course helps develop gross motor skills in addition to balance abilities and perception of space. Physical growth becomes better through active play because movement investigation builds self-confidence (Gagen & Getchell, 2023).

2. 0 to 12 months:

Activity Title: Sensory Basket Discovery
Activity: The shallow basket should contain several safe textured items such as a soft cloth and a wooden spoon and a silicone teether and crinkly paper. The infant needs both a soft mat for lying down and supportive sitting while reaching and touching di erent objects under adult supervision.
Outcome: Sensory Basket Discovery serves essential developmental areas for children between 0 to 12 months. Through this activity infants acquire cognitive competencies because they learn to detect multiple textures and colors and shapes which develops their problem-solving and cause-and-e ect learning abilities. Infant sensory growth becomes stronger while they touch various textures (soft, rough and smooth) and use rattles for audiotory engagement. The activity helps develop motor abilities by teaching infants to use their upper body to grasp objects and reach for them along with object manipulation. Through this activity language development occurs because caregivers teach new words while the baby learns to match sounds to physical objects. The social-emotional development of the infant improves because the activity creates a bonding experience which strengthens trust and security between the caregiver and child.

3.12 to 24 Months:

Activity: During picture book reading sessions the caregiver should point out images while naming visible objects.
Outcomes: During Storytime the children gain new words while learning to recognize connections between words and objects. The cognitive development of the child strengthens through recognition when they identify familiar objects and actions in pictures. Both the child and the caregiver create social bonds through this development activity while they read the book together.

2 to 3 years
Activity: Finger Painting
Childhood finger painting provides toddlers with creative satisfaction along with essential development of their sensory abilities and their hand function. Children can freely paint with non-toxic products either to draw specific pictures or to learn from touching and spreading paint across their hands. During paint activities youngsters develop their fine motor capabilities through their work with paint because this action aids them in grasping movements in addition to hand-eye coordination and finger skills. The activity supports cognitive learning through color experimentation which helps children understand cause e ect relationships when they notice how pressing their fingers makes marks in painted surfaces. The making of art during toddlerhood helps children share their feelings through painting activities.
Outcomes: The process of using fingers to paint teaches toddlers to control their hands while dealing with paint which develops perfect coordination between eyes and hands and strengthens finger muscles. The hands-on experience with mixtures while creating marks allows children to develop an understanding of cause and e ect and color relationships as well as cognitive abilities. The emotional aspect of finger painting enables toddlers to express their feelings through artwork which helps build their self-confidence as well as their expression abilities.
