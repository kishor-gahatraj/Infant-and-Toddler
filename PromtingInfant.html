Introduction of Promoting Infant and Toddler Development, Learning, and Wellbeing

The foundation for promoting development alongside learning and enhancing wellbeing among infants and toddlers depends on environments designed to help their emotional and cognitive and physical and social growth. Educators must practice responsive caregiving by observing individual child needs to develop secure attachments and positive emotional health (Berk & Meyers, 2023). Early childhood education organization relies on play-based learning as its essential framework to let children build their investigative practices and innovative thinking capabilities while learning to solve problems. The learning experiences prepared by educators enable children to build their language abilities and social competencies by providing meaningful peer connections (Department of Education, 2022). The Reggio Emilia and Pikler approaches formulate environments to enhance children's learner autonomy and promote both respect and their active learning roles (Gandini 2012; Gerber 2002). According to the Early Years Learning Framework (EYLF) educators need to construct a feeling of belonging and being alongside becoming to promote complete wellbeing as well as early developmental foundations (Department of Education, 2022) .
